# Glossary Summary

Purpose: Keep day-to-day terminology consistent while deferring authoritative definitions to specifications.

## Working Terms

- Source material: Original project material from which knowledge may be derived.
- Document: Stable project-scoped engineering record identity across revisions.
- Revision: Immutable historical state of a knowledge document.
- Fragment: Revision-bound, locator-addressable unit within a revision.
- Citation: Durable pointer to a revision or fragment.
- Assertion: Proposition about the project, distinct from raw source text.
- Observation: Human-originated record of what was perceived or measured.
- Requirement: Authoritative statement of what must, shall, or should occur.
- Constraint: Condition that limits valid states or actions.
- Rule: Executable deterministic logic derived from authoritative requirements or approved policy.
- Interpretation: Human or AI analysis of source material or observations, distinct from raw evidence.
- Proposal: Non-authoritative suggested content, relationship, or classification.
- Imported source: Immutable project-scoped registration of an original binary source plus its content identity.
- Storage object: Provider-neutral immutable storage identity for preserved original bytes.
- Import session: Durable batch-oriented record of one document import activity and its counts.
- Processing attempt: Durable record of one deterministic document-processing execution attempt.
- Document candidate: Non-authoritative processing output that may later be reviewed but is not authoritative knowledge by itself.
- Relationship: Semantic connection among documents, revisions, fragments, assertions, requirements, observations, reports, or proposals.
- Conflict: Explicit record of incompatible knowledge states that remains visible until resolved.
- Applicability: Explicit determination of what knowledge applies to a scope, asset, activity, time range, or report.
- Authority: Contextual basis for why a source or statement governs a specific question.
- Verification: Independent confirmation state separate from authority.
- Provenance: Traceable chain from source material through derived artifacts and human decisions.

## Authority

If a term is formally defined in a specification, that specification is authoritative.
Use this file as a quick-reference summary, not the source of truth.
